The Timeline

Five eras. One mission.

Vintage King grew alongside the rebirth of analog gear, the rise of the project studio, and the digital console era. Here is the short version of how we got from a basement to a global outfit.

1993 — 1999

The basement years.

Mike and Andrew Nehra start trading vintage gear out of a Detroit basement. The first deals are placed by phone and faxed list. The customers are working engineers who want gear that already has a story.

  • Founded as a vintage outboard and microphone dealer
  • First Neumann U47 and Fairchild 660 sales
  • Word-of-mouth network expands across the Midwest
2000 — 2005

From vintage dealer to outfitter.

The shop expands beyond vintage. New gear arrives — but only the gear engineers ask for. The first studio installations begin, the first Tech Shop opens, and the philosophy locks in: serve engineers like an engineer would.

  • First studio installations completed
  • Tech Shop opens — service for everything we sell
  • Inventory grows past 1,000 unique SKUs
2006 — 2011

The pro audio outfitter is born.

Vintage King becomes the destination for full studio builds. The audio consultant team grows, the warehouse expands, and the first Hall of Fame inductees get their dedicated pages. The brand stops being just a dealer and becomes an outfitter.

  • Audio Consultants team grows to engineer-led roster
  • Multi-room studio installations become a core service
  • Hall of Fame editorial program launched
2012 — 2015

The project studio era.

Project studios go pro. Vintage King builds the buyer guides, the trade-in program, and the financing options that put hero gear in home rooms. The Sell or Trade desk takes its place alongside the new gear floor.

  • Sell or Trade program standardized
  • VK Credit Card with extended financing launches
  • Buyer's guides and editorial deepen
2016 — 2018

The next 25 years.

Immersive audio, hybrid workflows, and a new generation of engineers reshape the room. Vintage King keeps the same job: serve the engineer behind the gear, whether the room is a basement or a billion-dollar facility.

  • Atmos and immersive build expertise scales
  • PLAYBACK Magazine and Make Your Mark editorial launches
  • Tech Shop service expands nationwide
